Chapter 2: Contributions

Invention of the 3-axis flight control system

The Wright brother's breakthrough was their invention of the three-axis control, which enabled the pilot to steer the aircraft with full control and enhanced the ability to easily maintain its equilibrium. This method became standard for all fixed-wing aircrafts.

Three-axis control system. 1902 Wright Glider.

The Lift Equation

They also derived the lift equation, an equation currently used to determine an aircraft's lifting capabilities till this day.

The lift equation states that lift L is equal to the lift coefficient Cl times the density r times half of the velocity V squared times the wing area A.
