Chapter 4: Life Events

High school without diplomas

Both brothers attended high school, but received no diplomas. The family's sudden move in 1884 from Richmond, Indiana, to Dayton, Ohio, where the family had lived during the 1870s, prevented Wilbur from receiving his diploma after finishing four years of high school.

Self-reliance

Orville dropped out of high school after his junior year to start a printing business in 1889, having designed and built his own printing press with Wilbur's help. Wilbur joined the print shop, and in March the brothers launched a weekly newspaper, the West Side News. In December 1892 the brothers opened a repair and sales shop (the Wright Cycle Exchange, later the Wright Cycle Company) and in 1896 began manufacturing their own brand.

Where it all began

In 1900, the brothers went to Kitty Hawk, North Carolina, to begin their manned gliding experiments after designing some gliders based on previous pioneers. After successfully manufacturing airplanes, they patented their inventions and their company (The Wright Company) set up a factory in Dayton and a flying school/test flight field at Huffman Prairie.
